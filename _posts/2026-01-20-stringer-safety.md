**Stringer Safety** is a safety companion for journalists working in high-risk environments.
It won **1st place** at the **AI Hack for Freedom** hackathon organized by the
**Human Rights Foundation (HRF)** and **Bitcoin Park**.

I worked on it with **Alex Lewin**, **Anjan Sundaram**,
[asraentr0py](https://x.com/asraentr0py), [sahilc0](https://x.com/sahilc0),
and [imyjimmy](https://x.com/imyjimmy).

The core idea is simple: when a journalist is under pressure, there is no time to
decide who to contact, what context to share, and how each person in the network can
help.

### What it does

- builds a trusted network of journalists, fixers, officials, embassy contacts, and family
- chronicles field updates so the support network has context before something goes wrong
- triggers a one-touch SOS that sends the right safety context to the right people
- uses **Maple**, HRF's encrypted AI tool, to suggest response actions in moments of danger
- points toward end-to-end encrypted location sharing and messaging as the product matures

### Why it matters

Frontline and freelance reporters often need to coordinate safety across countries,
time zones, and fragmented communication apps.
Stringer Safety compresses that operational burden into a tool designed for the moment
when seconds matter.

The current public site also makes clear that this is not just a panic button.
It is a broader safety workflow: prepare the network in advance, build context during
the assignment, and make emergency escalation immediate when something goes wrong.

The project is now being developed in partnership with **The Stringer Foundation**.
